What is Chocolate

All chocolate starts its life hanging on a tree. The scientific name of that tree is Theobroma cacao— Greek for “food of the gods” — but it is commonly referred to as a cacao or cocoa tree. These trees grow to be 30-40 feet tall, and produce small white flowers that later develop into fruit pods. Cacao fruits are typically 4-14 inches long and contain anywhere from 30-45 cacao beans surrounded by a white fleshy pulp.
